What is camera calibration?

Camera calibration determines your camera's internal characteristics, like focal length and distortion. This allows for accurate 3D measurements and projector mapping.

What do I need for calibration?

You need a printed checkerboard pattern (9Ã—6 inner corners). You can download and print one from our pattern generator, or use any standard chess/checkerboard with known square size.

How many images do I need?

We need at least 10 good images from different angles and distances. More images (up to 20) can improve accuracy. The system will guide you through capturing varied positions.

What makes a good calibration image?

Good images have: clear lighting, sharp focus, the entire pattern visible, varied angles and distances, and no motion blur. Our system provides real-time feedback to help you capture perfect images.

Troubleshooting camera issues

If your camera won't start: check browser permissions, try refreshing the page, ensure no other apps are using the camera, and try a different browser if needed.
